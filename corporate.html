Testing Report

  • Your testing goals – in other words, what you are trying to learn about your website?

    • Our testing had several goals. First, we wanted to learn if the website delivers user-friendly experience. While we were creating it, we wanted to achieve an easy to navigate design. As our website has very clear goal - to find a dog sitter- we wanted to know if the function is simple enough. Second, our website has the goal to connect dog owners, especially internationals in the Netherlands. By our research, we wanted to check if our "Blog page" helps people connect, share experiences and feel like part of a community. Finally, we wanted to achieve a high-quality outcome, so the user testing had a goal to guarantee an excellent product.

  • Your testing methods, which includes information about:
    • The test’s participants (number, match with target audience, etc.)

      • The testing was done with four participants. All of them are part of our target group - internationals and Dutch dog owners, aged above 18 years old. The people that took part in the testing were two Bulgarians, one Romanian, and one Dutch, aged 20, one 26, one 21 and one 28 accordingly. They are all owners of different kinds of dog breeds. Additionaly, all of the participants and currently living in the Netherlands.

    • The test’s setting (which material is used, is it done remotely, on campus, at home, etc.)

      • The testings were done in different ways. Three of them were done in person and one online, via Microsoft Teams. The ones that were conducted in person were done in various home locations. Three of the testings were conducted on a laptop and one on mobile phone. The questions were asked by one moderator.

    • The test’s protocol (what instructions are given, how it is recorded, etc.)

      • The testing started by asking each of the participants for approval to record. We voice-recorded all of the testings and took pictures of some of them. First we gave them a background information regarding our project:"For our university project, we, as second-year students studying Creative Business, have been assigned to develop a functional website targeted towards dog owners. One of our goals is to help internationals and Dutch dog owners to create a strong community and help each other."

      Then, we asked them to introduce themselves shortly - to say their name, age and nationality, so it can be clear that they are part of the target group. After that we asked them the following questions:

      What are your first impressions about this website?
      Can you find a dog sitter and book them, based on you dog characteristics?
      Can you find more info about dog walking?
      Can you add a new article in the blog?
      What are your overall thoughts about this website?
      Is the website easy to use?
      Is there something you would change?
      Would you use it yourself?

      Based on the received answers we asked additional questions.

Marketing

Context of campaign and promotional activities

Introduction

Doggify is a website that connects dog owners in the Netherlands. The main function of the website is to find suitable pet-sitting options for the dogs of mainly internationals, but Dutch people are also welcome to join the community. In return for 20 Euro per month, the customers have access to dog owners that offer temporary housing or pet care services. In addition to this, useful information can be found through interaction between dog owners in the form of a blog. This information is related to breed-specific questions or common problems such as housing, relatable to expats.

Vision

“In a world where emotional and practical support is rare to find away from home, we believe everyone deserves assistance in tough moments, because success is only built through teamwork.”

Mission

We as Doggify fight to make a drastic difference and reshape the idea of foreign big dog ownership, making it not just barely manageable but truly rewarding.

Values

Community: We aim to create a safe space where expat dog owners can not only bond over breed specifics but, above all, find a sense of belonging and make long-lasting friendships among kindred spirits.

Empathy: We understand the challenges that foreigners face when bringing their furry friends to a new country. Doggify is a platform that inspires them to connect and sympathize with each other. Our approach is to address their various needs with genuine care, ensuring a welcoming experience filled with understanding and warmth.

Shared Responsibility: We encourage big dog owners to actively contribute to the well-being of the community and its members. Doggify centers on collective action by offering dog care assistance, including sitting and walking, and sharing knowledge and resources.

Cultural Inclusivity: While Dutch organizations often use language and policies that inadvertently isolate internationals, at Doggify, we thrive in an international environment. Our platform welcomes all Dutch residents, regardless of background or origin, and deeply values diverse perspectives.

This report aims to define and explain the process of promoting the brand Doggify. It will describe in detail all the stages that the team went through to communicate persuasively the brand to its target audience.

Communication Message and objectives

Target audience

Doggify’s target audience includes Gen X, Millennials, and Older Gen Z between the ages of 18-60. Doggify is mostly orientated toward international expats living in The Netherlands and owning one or more than one dog. While Dutch citizens are also welcome to join, we advertise the brand specifically as one tailored to the needs of internationals. The reason is that when you search up other companies on the Internet like Petbnb, Pawshake, etc., they are all in Dutch. This excludes a whole customer segment which makes up nearly 15% of the Dutch population, or 2.5 million people in total (Netherlands, n.d.). 18% of Dutch households have a dog (GlobalPETS, 2023). This means that 450 000 expat dog owners in The Netherlands are not targeted with any specific brand/product yet. This gives us an opportunity for a highly lucrative business.

What unites our target audience is dog ownership and migration from abroad. As we aim to create a community, we will not segment that target audience per age and dog size/breed. We want the target audience to be broad enough to be populated by the brand to as many customers as possible. This, however, means we have to take into account all the differences between generations so we can advertise in the most effective way.

Ou (2020) discusses the different perceptions of the dog in different generations. Younger generations like Millennials and Gen Z accept the dog as their child - preparation for their real child. While older generations like Gen X consider the dog as a ‘’valued family member’’. All generations unite under the benefit of the dogs as reducers of stress, loneliness, and anxiety. Based on the results of the research of Megna (2024), Millennials and Gen X are leading in the percentage of pet owners, in contrast to Gen Z who are in the last position after the Baby Boomers. Furthermore, Kelly (2024) discusses some of the reasons for this result. According to her article, the current economy and job market are sources of anxiety for Gen Z. Another reason is that some of the presenters of this generation are still studying at university which leads to lower income. In most cases, they rent an accommodation. The rules of some landlords who don’t allow pets, also influence the decision-making for Gen Z.

Generation X

Generation X are born between 1965 and 1980, so they are around 50 years old. Kotler and Armstrong (2020) describe this generation as skeptical buyers and not very easily influenced by marketing pitches. On the other hand, they prefer quality over quantity. They also are very loyal customers once they find the brand they like. Most Gen X are pet owners which can be explained because they are in their careers and many of them own a home with a growing family. In other words, they have a stable lifestyle which led to taking care of dogs. Also, most of the presenters of Gen X are parents of already grown-up children. Kretzler et al. (2022) discusses the positive relationship between loneliness and pet ownership. That's the reason that some Gen X's take dogs to not feel lonely after their children move away.

Millennials

Millennials are born between 1981 and 1995, so most of them are in their 30s. Kotler and Armstrong (2020) illustrate Millennials as a digitally comfortable generation. They are the first generation that grew up in a fully digital world. That is the reason that they can easily engage with brands on social media. However, due to rapid changes in the global economy, world events such as COVID-19, and several financial crises throughout the years, only about a third of millennials globally are financially secure (Millennials Are Falling Into a Depression Over Their Finances, 2023).

Generation Z

Generation Z was born between 1996 and 2012, so the people who are included in the target audience would be in their 20s. This is the generation that doesn’t know a world without the Internet. Hedges (2024) comments that social media is central to the life of Gen Z people. They shape their perspective through social media consumption. Some evidence for that is that “39% of Gen Z girls find shopping inspirations from social media.” (Kotler & Armstrong, 1972). Another characteristic of Gen Z is their free spirit and sense of exploration. Hedges (2024b) states that this generation likes to travel and it’s their priority compared to previous generations. That makes it difficult for people of those ages to take care of a dog because sometimes it’s difficult to travel with it or just to leave it to someone. Expect the unstable financial state and living conditions, the urge for traveling explains the low percentage of dog owners amongst this generation.

Platforms

In 2024, there are estimated to be 5.04 billion total social media users worldwide” (Statista, 2024). The image below illustrates this percentage compared to Internet users.

However, out of those 5.04 billion, Facebook is considered the most used social media worldwide. According to Wiley (2022), 75% of Generation X use social media. The main preference amongst their presenters is Facebook, followed by YouTube. On the other hand, ‘’Millennials are the most active demographic on social media” (Zote, 2024). The top 3 most used social media platforms by this generation are Facebook, Instagram and YouTube. Zote (2024) commented that Gen Z spent daily the most time out of all generations on social media. They may have visited in the last few years TikTok the most, but as their favorite social media, they chose Instagram.

Communication objectives

Reach Objective

To create a community where international dog owners interact with each other we need at least 50-100 initial website users so this can be possible. A survey made by McKinsey showed that only 55% of all people who engage with a service actually end up subscribing, so half of those engaged should also be subscribed to our website (Ristagno, 2020).

This is why we aim to be seen by 800 people, 200 of whom are highly engaged with our content, so at least 50-100 of them will sign up for our website.

Affect

We aim to create content that evokes certain emotional reactions in our target audience. We want them to feel informed about the brand and be inspired by seeing a representation of them - expats with dogs. We want to pique their curiosity about the brand. They need to feel certain that our brand is something that doesn’t exist anywhere else because it is created specifically to help them and no one else. They need to feel like they are part of a community of like-minded individuals, that they are always supported by this community, and they can trust it, and that this is not just a money-oriented company but a platform where they finally belong. They need to feel excited to make new friendships and exchange tips & tricks with other international dog lovers.

Our team has produced those objectives based on the SMART method. Our goals are:

Specific: As mentioned, we have set a specific number of followers, engagement, reach, and subscription, and trying to evoke certain emotions in customers.

Measurable: We aim to be seen by 800 people, 200 of whom are highly engaged with our content, so at least 50-100 of them will sign up for our website.

Achievable: Our goals are realistic as they are not too high, and they are backed up by the aforementioned sources.

Relevant: The mentioned emotional reactions we want to cause are tightly related to our brand’s vision and values. Moreover, the number of followers, engagement, and reach we have set as an objective will lead to people subscribing to our website. The visuals planned to be incorporated into the social media content are stylistically similar to the overall look of the website.

Time-based: The end date of achieving our goals would be 1st April. Then we would start analysing the data based on the content was created from the beginning of the accounts. After the data is analysed, we would improve the campaign for the upcoming months.

Response

We aim to make the target audience consume our content and actively engage with it- this means liking, commenting, sharing, and reposting our publications. This leads to our ultimate objective - getting people to sign up for Doggify’s website as a response to our content.

Learning Points

Based on an almost 1-month social campaign that our team organized to raise awareness related to Doggify, we can come to several conclusions. Firstly, all objectives were reached so the campaign could be considered successful. Furthermore, Facebook and Instagram profiles reach slightly more followers than it was set. Secondly, the reach on Facebook was bigger than on Instagram. The team did not expect that difference because of the popularity of Instagram among society. Moreover, it can be said that Facebook has a more precise algorithm on the base of the high reach.

Thirdly, the team successfully justified the choice of social media platforms based on the age of the target group. Younger audiences prefer Instagram and people above 35 years are more active on Facebook.

As a point of improvement, it can be mentioned in terms of the creation of content that more men must be attracted to the platform. It is visible that the main audience of the brand is women above 75% on both platforms. That need to be considered in the future planning of the brand.

To sum up, the social media campaign was considered as every member of the team as successful, but there is always space for improvement. That's way in the future we will try to increase the reach and the engagement of the users. In addition, the team would try to increase the engagement rate of men and balance the chart between both genders.

We also learned that social media is quite unpredictable. We had carefully planned all posts beforehand and some of them were quite similar, but they had different outcomes. For instance, we posted two community videos on Instagram. Their content was quite similar, only different sounds. While one of them gained only 700 views, the other gained 1500+ views and 100+ likes. We learned that sound usage is crucial in reel-making, and that original sounds tend to get fewer views rather than widely used ones.

Associating with similar brands

At some point in the campaign, Doggify was promoted from a similar brand for dog services. That was a good act for the brand image to be associated with a bigger brand. Also, it attracts more followers for both parties, therefore it is better for creating a community.

Giveaway

Doggify’s giveaway was in the form of 3 months free subscription and a Pookie plushie. The giveaway was created to increase the followers and engagement of the brand. While the other post users could scroll and just see it, if they participate, they need to put more effort into following the account, like the post, and tag a friend in the comments section. In addition, this increases brand awareness because they tag friends which leads to a snowball effect.

Getting to know the target audience

We posted stories on both Facebook and Instagram which included questions about our customers - how often they travel, what size their dog is, etc. We did this for three reasons. Firstly, this creates more engagement as the target audience will click on an option in each poll. Secondly, this is good for our brand image as it has a personal note- when the target audience notices we are interested in them as individuals, they are way more likely to engage with the brand and buy its products. This is also tightly connected to our main objective-building a sense of community. Thirdly, the results from those polls helped us analyze our target audience and the viability of the website.

The results were quite similar to the findings from the problem and solution interviews- most international dog owners have a busy schedule, travel relatively often, and need pet-sitting services once a week. This is a good sign for our product, as it is something that the target audience actually needs.

Moreover, we made an Instagram and Facebook story where the target audience could ask us questions. This aimed to alleviate the experience for the target audience as communication was now two-sided- we are not only interested in them, but we also are open to listening and communicating with them. This two-sided communication helps build a sense of community. The questions asked were subsequently answered through a Facebook and Instagram post, and later added as a “Frequently asked questions” section Doggify’s website.

  • Lean Canvas #2

    • Problem

    • Lack of trust-worthy people who would take care of the dog: Consumers are the most concerned with finding someone to take care of their dog when they are away from home, sick, or with extremely busy schedules.
    • Feeling of loneliness: No specialized communities here in the Netherlands where internationals can share, exchange, and socialize regarding their dogs. Either for daily dog activities or struggles.
    • Finding accommodation: Typical Dutch rental properties prohibit pets, which drastically restricts the options for dog owners to find accommodation.
    • Language barrier in pet-sitting services: Internationals are unable to fully engage or understand the all-in Dutch dog services as they haven’t learned Dutch yet. This makes their accessing potential solutions hard to make use of.

    Solution

    • Create a blog function where customers can share their struggles, and others can offer solutions, including providing them with resources about pet-friendly rental properties: This feature would allow for an exchange of valuable information and experiences among pet owners, helping them navigate the challenges of finding pet-friendly accommodations and other dog-related issues.
    • Establish specialized dog owner communities where everybody feels welcome and deeply values diverse perspectives: Such communities would foster a sense of belonging and support among dog owners, encouraging the sharing of advice, experiences, and resources that cater to the unique needs of diverse dog owners.
    • A dog community platform entirely in English, ensuring inclusivity and ending any uneasiness of feeling left out due to a language barrier: By providing a platform in English, internationals and non-Dutch speakers can fully participate and benefit from the community, ensuring that information and support are accessible to everyone, regardless of their language proficiency.

    Customer segments

    • Foreign expats with one or multiple dogs living in the Netherlands. All ages: This target group includes internationals from various backgrounds and ages, all sharing the commonality of living in the Netherlands with their canine companions.
    • Owners of all types of breeds – small, medium-sized, and big: The service or product caters to dog owners regardless of the breed size, ensuring inclusivity and versatility in addressing the needs of all dogs.
    • Brand Persona: Gergana is a 28-year-old expat in the Netherlands: She has a very busy schedule due to her job as a nurse at Erasmus University Medical Center. Despite having a relatively small circle of friends, she is an outgoing person. Describing herself as a creative and active person, one of Gergana’s biggest strengths is her organization skills which allows her to manage her time effectively amidst her busy lifestyle with a packed agenda. Teamwork is not something unusual to her, and she works effectively with other people. Gergana prioritizes safety rather than taking risks.
    • This excludes a whole customer segment which makes up nearly 15% of the Dutch population, or 2.5 million people in total (Netherlands, n.d.). 18% of Dutch households have a dog (GlobalPETS, 2023): This means that 450,000 expat dog owners in The Netherlands are not targeted with any specific brand/product yet. This gives us an opportunity for a highly lucrative business.

    Unique value proposition

    • International Friendly: Compared to other pet-sitting companies especially dog ones in the Netherlands, our services and the information we provide our customers with are entirely in English. Whereas when you search for other companies on the internet like Petbnb, Pawshake, etc. they seem to be in Dutch which makes the experience for internationals even harder.
    • Easy-to-use subscription model: It sets us apart, as we understand that convenience is important and that’s why we’ve used a very simple and hassle-free subscription model. With just a few clicks, you can be a member and start enjoying our services without any difficulties.
    • Creating a community: What truly sets us apart is that we are committed to creating a community where dog owners feel free, helpful, and a sense of belonging. This community will offer you a space where you can connect with like-minded individuals all in English, so no discrimination.

    Unfair advantage

    • Networking Opportunities: We can easily target international communities in the Netherlands such as Romanian, Bulgarian, Hungarian, etc. as those people have already formed close circles, and societies and are open to international services.
    • Diverse Environment
    • Specialized Services: Dog walking, daily visits and sittings, overnight sitting.
    • Blog Function: A space where everybody is free to ask their questions to like-minded individuals with the same issues, and have an opportunity to add an article of his own to a topic related to dogs.

    Services/products

    Doggify is a website that connects all the international dog guardians in the Netherlands. For just 20 per month, dog owners get access to owners who offer temporary housing or pet care services. The best part is that dog owners get to make a friend who can show them a new cool calming trick, or maybe even explore the Dutch landscape with them. It’s all about community!

    It contributes to the value proposition specifically because of customization. Our product is precisely tailored to the specific needs of our target audience. After conducting several interviews and research on the topic, we concluded that international dog owners in the Netherlands don’t have a community where they could share their challenges, and seek mutual support regarding their dog. It gives a helpful solution to their worries, and we’ve taken into account many of them to create Doggify and make it as useful as possible.

    The fit between the product and the brand identity is deemed strong. After conducting all of the interviews, all of the interviewees found Doggify unique and helpful for every international dog owner who struggles with finding a suitable dog daycare, or just a friend with whom they could share the same experience/challenges. They all thought that Doggify evokes a sense of community and inclusivity with its blog feature, and “English only” policy. Doggify’s brand identity represents exactly that. All of our interviews revealed that people are excited about the launch of the website, and would love to use it one day, as it solves a lot of their challenges like finding someone to take care of their dog while they’re on holiday, back home, or just having a busy daily life, and don’t have time to walk their dog. In conclusion, we can say that Doggify perfectly aligns with their needs and preferences.

    Conclusions interviews 

     All of the interviewees fit our brand persona. They’re all international dog owners living in the Netherlands, between the ages of 20 and 41, so we cover a big age group. They’re nationalities vary from Bulgarians, Romanians, Serbian, Slovenian, Belgium, etc.


    Problem interviews 

      They all face similar problems – from struggling to find a place for their dog to stay when they’re traveling, to challenges to find other international dog owners, and befriending them. They all need support from people with a mutual lifestyle and also wish that there is a space where they can exchange tips and tricks regarding their dogs, and have trust in the people they’re leaving their dogs at. Other than that they want to socialize and be a part of something, because they’re usually with a very busy schedule and don’t have time to go out and meet new people. As they’re internationals and their main communication language here is English, they often face challenges with pet-sitting websites as many of them are in Dutch, and even if they also have an English version, most of the people there are older and speak only Dutch.

     
    Solution interviews 

      When doing the solution interviews, we got feedback on our demo, and potential ideas for changes on the real product. While facing some challenges while navigating the website, they still thought that it’s easy to use and provides a lot of the needed information. The overall opinion is that they find the blog page hard to navigate, that some of the buttons were not available for example “add comment”, or finding where to ask questions. Those were little details that didn’t really bother their overall opinion and navigation through the website. The colors they associate with the website are either light brown or dark brown-ish, with the reason that they think of many dogs’ fur. Several also mentioned the color blue, which they associate with safety because they want to feel safe when they leave their dog with someone else. The interviewees think that the subscription fee is too low, and that we should increase it. The overall opinion is that the website provides people with something that is not yet on the market and that they would use it. 


    Pivot 

    Our subscription price at first was €10. We thought that as a new brand going on the market, we shouldn’t start with a high subscription fee. As an unknown brand, we think that we still don’t have the audience’s trust and support and that we need to prove ourselves as worthy and qualitative of their trust and finances. During the testing of our MVP and the interviews, all of us were met with a totally different perception of the audience. Their opinion was that with all of the options, and opportunities we give to international dog owners in the Netherlands, we should charge an average of €30 to €40. Considering how expensive only a night stay for dogs at specialized hotels is, they think that a higher price is more suitable. Many internationals need our service, and the inclusivity we promote, so in their opinion increasing the price won’t make the chances for more subscribers low. We decided that it’s best to increase the price by €10, and make it €20, and not be as cheap. This will also help our revenue streams because as a new brand, we can never be sure of all the costs that we will have on the way. During our Production user testing, we increased the price, and all the testers didn’t mind the price of €20 per month. They were satisfied with the result and thought it was the perfect fit for price quality.
