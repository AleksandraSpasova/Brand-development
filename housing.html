Finding a Rental in the Netherlands
with a Furry Friend

As a pet owner in the Netherlands, finding a rental property that also welcomes your dog can be a nightmare. With the country facing a housing shortage and landlords often preferring tenants without pets, keeping up with the rental market feels impossible. 

 Now, I won’t lie to you- your chances are not very high. That’s why most dog owners buy properties - an insane but also impressive strategy to get you into debt. However, with the right approach and a few useful strategies, you can increase the likelihood of securing a pet-friendly rental for you and your furry friend.

It Is Never Early Enough

I know that you’re thinking, “I don’t even know how things work here, how am I supposed to just find a place”. Trust me, I’ve been there. The first thing you can do is take advantage of online rental platforms and websites that allow you to sort your search results based on pet-friendly criteria. Such filters are frequently available on websites like Funda, Pararius, and Kamernet. 

 Additionally, consider joining Facebook and Instagram groups or online forums dedicated to pet owners in the Netherlands, as members often share listings. It is enough to type some keywords in the search engine, and the groups will immediately pop up - make sure to check them every day. What most people nowadays miss when searching for accommodation is networking. If you know just one person in The Netherlands, you already have connections! Text them and ask them for tips and direct insights. Who knows - maybe they’re leaving their room soon, and you’re the perfect new tenant!

Utilize Online Resources

Take advantage of online rental platforms and websites that allow you to filter search results based on pet-friendly criteria. Websites like Funda, Pararius, and Kamernet often have filters specifically for pet-friendly properties. Additionally, consider joining social media groups or online forums dedicated to pet owners in the Netherlands, as members may share valuable insights and listings.

Prepare a Pet Resume

Consider creating a pet résumé to submit with your rental application to show that you are a responsible pet owner. Provide details about your dog's breed, size, temperament, and immunization history. You can also include references from past landlords or neighbors who can vouch for the behavior of your pet.

Provide Proof of Training

Make sure you provide your potential landlord with documented proof that your dog has completed obedience training or received certification as a therapy or service animal. Seeing it on paper will soothe all reservations, and your landlord will be convinced your dog is well-trained and behaved.

While finding a pet-friendly rental in the Netherlands can be challenging, many dog owners have already done it. With patience, persistence, and, most importantly, a proactive approach, it is possible to secure suitable accommodations for you and your furry friend. 

Good luck!
